My Projects

Project 1 - Blood Pressure Checker Code

During my time at CUNY SPS, there's one project that really stands out. It's not the most complex or impressive piece of code, but it's special to me because it's the first time I felt like coding could truly simplify life. This project is a simple Python program—a blood pressure checker.

The idea behind it was simple too: create a tool that anyone, especially those not familiar with medical jargon, could use to monitor their blood pressure. We wanted to make it easy, especially for older folks who might find traditional blood pressure readings confusing.

Here's how it works: you input your systolic and diastolic blood pressure readings, the two key numbers for assessing your blood pressure health. The program then gives you a straightforward, easy-to-understand interpretation.

If your blood pressure is in the healthy range, you get a reassuring message like "Your blood pressure is normal. Keep up the good work!" It's a small thing, but it can bring peace of mind to anyone worried about their health. But if the readings suggest a potential health issue, the program gently advises you to consult a doctor. Sometimes, that gentle nudge can be lifesaving.

For me, this project was a turning point. It was the first time I created code that felt genuinely practical and meaningful in the real world. It wasn't the most advanced code, but it showed me the incredible potential of coding. It's not just about algorithms and syntax; it's about solving real-world problems and making a positive impact.

As I look back on this project, I can't help but smile. It might be a simple beginner's project, but it reminds me that even small coding projects can have a big impact. It taught me that coding is a tool for simplifying complex aspects of life, one line of code at a time.

So, if you ever find yourself struggling with a blood pressure monitor or facing any other everyday challenge, remember that coding can be the key to making life a little bit easier. It's not just about the code itself; it's about the difference it can make in people's lives..

Project 2 - Package Delivery Innovation

Jumping into this school project was like a cool adventure, imagining what-if scenarios with the Local Express Delivery Company as our creative sandbox. We pretended it started back in 2003 and used it to dig into how drones could shake up the package delivery game. I got really into picturing a future where drones are buzzing around instead of delivery trucks, especially as tech gets better every day. We looked at how Amazon is doing things and dreamed up a scene where drones are zipping through the sky, dropping off packages in far-off places super fast—like a 13-minute delivery run in Cambridgeshire.

I dived into the money-saving and green benefits of our made-up drone program, kind of like the big dreams tech whizzes have. The project had me figuring out all the nitty-gritty, like how much stuff costs, training people to fly drones, and dealing with all the rules about flying. I cooked up solutions that felt like the real tough choices businesses face, like shifting budgets around and changing what jobs people do. It was all about thinking ahead and making smart moves.

But this project was more than just coming up with strategies; it was a story about rolling with the punches and what the future of work might look like. It gave us the chance to write a new chapter on how business and caring for the planet can go hand in hand, mixing serious study with wild creativity that you usually get to play with in school.

Looking back, it's clear that we weren't just guessing about what a company's future might hold; we were also getting our heads around how progress works, blending new ideas with what actually makes sense. Even though we made up the strategies and tech, it showed how school can get us pumped to think up new ideas. This wasn't just homework; it was a journey through imagination and the kind of forward-thinking that pushes us forward.

Project 3 - Project Management Simulation

Reflecting on the simulation of "Project Tech at Bankers.x” in my project management studies, I'm filled with a renewed understanding and appreciation for the complexities of managing large-scale projects. This assignment wasn't just an academic exercise; it was an immersive simulation that brought to life the realities and challenges of project management, resonating deeply with my passion for the field.

The scenario presented in this simulation was both daunting and exhilarating: outfitting a rebuilt floor with the latest technology for 300 staff, all within a stringent four-week deadline. The thought of managing a simulated budget of $750,000 added a realistic edge to the exercise, challenging me to think critically about resource allocation and cost management.

I was particularly intrigued by the strategic division of the virtual IT team into specialized groups, each handling aspects like Computing & Accessories, Networking, Telecommunication, and Security. Observing how these groups navigated their tasks in the simulation, adapted to changes, and collaborated was an enlightening insight into the importance of teamwork and specialization in project management.

Progressing through the simulated weeks of the project was a rollercoaster of learning and adaptation. The early phases, marked by rapid advancements, contrasted sharply with the challenges and roadblocks encountered later on, pushing the limits of my problem-solving skills. The extension week in the simulation, where the Computing & Accessories and Security groups faced and overcame technical issues, was particularly instructive.

That additional week, though part of a hypothetical scenario, was crucial in underscoring the essence of teamwork and determination. The way the team in the simulation rallied together to address compatibility issues, enhance security protocols, and ensure seamless integration of systems was a powerful lesson in the effectiveness of collaborative efforts.

Despite the simulated nature, facing challenges such as technical glitches and scope expansion, which led to a budget overrun in the scenario, taught me the importance of clear and proactive communication in project management.

Overall, this simulation of Project Tech was more than just an academic assignment; it was an enlightening journey into the heart of project management. It emphasized adaptability, clear communication, and the significance of a cohesive team approach. As a student passionate about project management, this simulated experience was incredibly valuable, offering practical insights and reinforcing my belief in the power of strategic planning and teamwork in overcoming project challenges.

Project 4 - Case Study: Travel Agent VS Self Planning Vacation

Planning my vacation trip as a class assignment was an exhilarating blend of fun and practicality. Titled "Vacation Trip Planning," this project entailed mapping out a detailed journey across several European cities, including Lisbon, Madrid, and Paris, on a budget of $8,500. As I immersed myself in this project, I found a unique joy in the meticulous process of organizing each aspect of the trip - from choosing flights and accommodations to picking dining spots and sightseeing activities.

I compared the traditional use of a travel agent with the more modern, hands-on approach of self-planning. While a travel agent would cost around $250, self-planning amounted to roughly $120 for six hours of my time. This aspect of the project wasn't just enjoyable; it was a real eye-opener about the cost-effectiveness and personalization possible in trip planning today.

Diving into the world of online resources like Google, TripAdvisor, and Kayak, I realized how technology has revolutionized the travel planning process. It's fascinating how accessible and efficient trip planning has become, thanks to the internet. This project, while a fun academic exercise, turned out to be a practical demonstration of navigating the digital travel landscape.

However, it wasn't without its challenges. Balancing the elements of the trip required considerable effort and time. It brought into perspective the value of travel agents, especially for those who might find the process overwhelming or time-consuming.

In summary, this project was an adventure in itself, blending the excitement of creating a personalized travel experience with the real-world applicability of digital tools and self-planning. It was a journey through the intricacies of travel logistics, revealing both the joys and challenges of modern vacation planning.
