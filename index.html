My Projects

Project 1

During my time at CUNY SPS, there's one project that really stands out. It's not the most complex or impressive piece of code, but it's special to me because it's the first time I felt like coding could truly simplify life. This project is a simple Python program—a blood pressure checker.

The idea behind it was simple too: create a tool that anyone, especially those not familiar with medical jargon, could use to monitor their blood pressure. We wanted to make it easy, especially for older folks who might find traditional blood pressure readings confusing.

Here's how it works: you input your systolic and diastolic blood pressure readings, the two key numbers for assessing your blood pressure health. The program then gives you a straightforward, easy-to-understand interpretation.

If your blood pressure is in the healthy range, you get a reassuring message like "Your blood pressure is normal. Keep up the good work!" It's a small thing, but it can bring peace of mind to anyone worried about their health. But if the readings suggest a potential health issue, the program gently advises you to consult a doctor. Sometimes, that gentle nudge can be lifesaving.

For me, this project was a turning point. It was the first time I created code that felt genuinely practical and meaningful in the real world. It wasn't the most advanced code, but it showed me the incredible potential of coding. It's not just about algorithms and syntax; it's about solving real-world problems and making a positive impact.

As I look back on this project, I can't help but smile. It might be a simple beginner's project, but it reminds me that even small coding projects can have a big impact. It taught me that coding is a tool for simplifying complex aspects of life, one line of code at a time.

So, if you ever find yourself struggling with a blood pressure monitor or facing any other everyday challenge, remember that coding can be the key to making life a little bit easier. It's not just about the code itself; it's about the difference it can make in people's lives..

Project 2

Embarking on this school project has been a remarkable journey of imagination, a venture into the realm of'what could be' with the Local Express Delivery Company serving as our visionary playground. Established in the theoretical world of 2003, this company provided a fascinating case study to explore the transformative potential of drone technology in the courier industry.

Through this project, I was swept up in the narrative of a future where buzzing drones replace the familiar sight of delivery trucks, a future that feels closer with each technological leap. Inspired by the real-world progress of companies like Amazon, we created a mock scenario where drones zip across the sky, making deliveries to remote locations with impressive efficiency—envisioning a package’s journey that's as quick as the one in Cambridgeshire, completed in just 13 minutes.

Delving into the environmental and financial implications of our simulated drone integration, I found myself immersed in the dual goals of cost-cutting and sustainability. It was an engaging exercise to theorize about the potential savings and environmental benefits, mirroring the ambitions and predictions of tech pioneers.

This academic exercise pushed me to tackle the intricate details of cost analysis, drone operation training, and the web of regulatory compliance that governs the skies. Our proposed solutions, crafted from the ground up in our classrooms, reflected the tough decisions that stir real businesses to the core. From reallocating budgets to redefining job roles, each hypothetical step was steeped in strategy and foresight.

More than just a strategy paper, this project has been a narrative about adaptation and the future of work. It was an opportunity to script a new chapter in the story of commerce and environmental consciousness, blending the academic rigor of policy and management with the unbridled creativity that academic exercises allow.

As I look back on the project, it's clear to me that we weren't just hypothesizing about the future of a company; we were learning about the delicate dance of progress, where innovation meets practicality. Even though this story is a tapestry of fictional strategies and speculative technologies, it embodies our scholastic exploration of the skies—an illustration of how education can inspire us to dream up the next chapter of our collective journey. This was not just an assignment; it was a narrative spun from the threads of possibility and the spirit of innovation that drives us all.
