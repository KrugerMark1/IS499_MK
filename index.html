About Me

I was born in Argentina, grew up in New York City, and attended school in the Bronx. I embrace my Argentine roots when watching football (soccer) and when I find myself craving our traditional dishes. I'm an avid lover of video games, movies, and dining out at diverse restaurants. I've traveled to 15 cities across 10 states, always eager to explore and learn about different places.

Throughout my journey, I've taken on some cool projects. I whipped up a simple Python code to check out blood pressure readings, making it a handy tool for folks to gauge their health. On another note, I sketched out a proposal to help USPS deliver packages quicker and cheaper – I'm all about refining everyday processes. I've also dabbled in project management, working on a simulation that gave me a sneak peek into the intricacies of that world. And lastly, on the fun-er side of projects, I planned out an entire dream vacation in a case study, weighing the pros of travel agencies against the adventure of going solo.

Currently, I play a pivotal role in a financial company that houses 10,000 employees. I supervise a fantastic team of 25, ensuring everything received in the building is accurately accounted for and promptly dispatched. It’s like playing Tetris – but instead of dropping blocks, I handle packages, ensuring they fit neatly within the logistics.
